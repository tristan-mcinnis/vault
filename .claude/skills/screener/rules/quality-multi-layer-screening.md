# Multi-Layer Attitudinal Screening

Attitudinal criteria ("resonate with", "connect to", "align with") require 2-3 questions to validate. Never assume awareness equals resonance.

## Three-Layer Framework

| Layer | Question Type | Purpose |
|-------|--------------|---------|
| 1. Awareness | Scale (1-5) | Do they know about it? |
| 2. Understanding | Open-ended | Can they interpret it correctly? |
| 3. Resonance | Scale (1-5) | Does it connect personally? |

## Example: Brand Spirit Screening

Criteria: "Respondents who resonate with brand X's spirit"

**Layer 1 - Awareness:**
> How familiar are you with brand X? (1=Not at all, 5=Very familiar)
> Requirement: Score ≥ 3

**Layer 2 - Understanding:**
> In your own words, what does brand X stand for?
> Recruiter validation: Mentions [key brand values]

**Layer 3 - Resonance:**
> How much does brand X's spirit resonate with you personally? (1=Not at all, 5=Very much)
> Requirement: Score ≥ 4

## Conditional Routing

Verify prior state exists before asking about change.

| Bad | Good |
|-----|------|
| Q16 includes "0 purchases", Q17 asks "Why fewer?" | Q17 only appears IF Q16 ≥ 1 |
| Ask about brand switch without confirming they switched | Confirm switch happened, then ask why |

## Avoid

| Pattern | Problem |
|---------|---------|
| Single-question attitudinal screening | Conflates awareness with resonance |
| Assuming stated preference = actual behavior | Need behavioral validation |
| Asking about change without establishing baseline | Invalid comparison |
