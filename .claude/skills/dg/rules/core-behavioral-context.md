# Behavioral Context Framework

For purchase-related or innovation research, ensure the DG probes these contextual dimensions. Missing any creates blind spots in the trade-up or switching story.

## The 5 Dimensions

| Dimension | Why It Matters | Where to Probe |
| --------- | -------------- | -------------- |
| **Channel** | Where they buy shapes what they see/consider | Brand/competition section |
| **Format/pack size** | Single-serve vs. sharing affects value perception | Concept evaluation (price probe) |
| **Frequency** | Daily habit vs. occasional treat = different value logic | Occasions section |
| **Social context** | Solo vs. shared affects which benefits matter | Concept evaluation or occasions |
| **Discovery** | How they find new products = launch implications | Brand/competition (if in scope) |

## Dimension Deep Dives

### Channel
**Questions to answer:**
- Where do they typically buy?
- Online vs. offline split?
- Retailer preferences and why?
- How does channel affect consideration set?

**Strategic value:** Launch channel prioritization, retail partner strategy

### Format/Pack Size
**Questions to answer:**
- What sizes do they buy and when?
- Single-serve vs. multipack vs. sharing size?
- How does format affect price sensitivity?

**Strategic value:** SKU rationalization, price-pack architecture

### Frequency
**Questions to answer:**
- How often do they purchase/consume?
- Routine vs. occasion-driven?
- Has frequency changed over time?

**Strategic value:** Demand forecasting, loyalty vs. acquisition strategy

### Social Context
**Questions to answer:**
- Solo consumption vs. shared?
- Who else is involved in purchase/consumption?
- Does social context affect brand choice?

**Strategic value:** Positioning (personal indulgence vs. sharing), pack design

### Discovery
**Questions to answer:**
- How do they learn about new products?
- Role of recommendations, social media, in-store?
- What triggers trial?

**Strategic value:** Launch marketing, media mix, sampling strategy

## Integration Principle

**Don't create a standalone "purchase behavior" section.** Weave these into existing sections as probes or sub-questions:

| Dimension | Natural Home |
| --------- | ------------ |
| Channel | Brand/competition section |
| Format | Price evaluation within concept testing |
| Frequency | Occasion mapping |
| Social context | Concept evaluation or occasions |
| Discovery | Brand/competition (if in scope) |

## Example Integration

### In Brand/Competition Section
```
Q: Which brands do you buy most often in this category?
    - Where do you typically buy them? [Channel]
    - How do you usually discover new products? [Discovery]
```

### In Occasion Mapping
```
Q: Walk me through when you typically enjoy [product].
    - How often would that be? [Frequency]
    - Is that usually alone or with others? [Social context]
```

### In Concept Evaluation
```
Q: At [price], would you consider buying this?
    - What size would you expect at that price? [Format]
    - Would you buy this for yourself or to share? [Social context]
```

## Validation Checklist

Before finalizing a DG for purchase/innovation research:

- [ ] Channel is probed (where do they buy?)
- [ ] Format/size context captured (at least in price probes)
- [ ] Frequency established (daily, weekly, occasional?)
- [ ] Social context explored (solo vs. shared)
- [ ] Discovery mechanism understood (if relevant to launch)

If any are missing, add as probes to existing questions—don't create a new section.
