# Concept / Stimulus Testing Protocol

When a DG includes concept evaluation, stimulus reaction, or product testing, apply these design rules. They prevent common gaps where unaided expectations go uncaptured, behavioral context is missed, or sensory language isn't built before evaluation.

## 1. Pre-Stimulus Capture (Mandatory)

Before showing ANY stimulus material, capture respondents' unaided expectations. This creates a diagnostic baseline to compare concepts against and prevents concept language from anchoring their thinking.

### Principle
**Unaided before aided.** Always ask "what would you want?" before showing "here's what we made."

### Format by Method

| Method | When | Format |
| ------ | ---- | ------ |
| IDI | End of brand/competition section, before concepts | Verbal: "Describe your ideal [product] for [their occasion]" |
| FGD | End of brand section, before break | Written on cards: individual exercise, collected by moderator |

### What to Capture
- Desired taste, texture, ingredients, format
- Occasion fit
- Emotional payoff
- Use THEIR vocabulary from earlier sections

### Why It Matters
Without pre-stimulus capture:
- Cannot diagnose whether concepts meet expectations
- Concept language anchors respondent thinking
- Miss opportunity to hear unprimed vocabulary

## 2. Behavioral Context Checklist

For purchase-related or innovation research, ensure the DG probes these contextual dimensions. Missing any creates blind spots in the trade-up or switching story.

| Dimension | Why It Matters | Where to Probe |
| --------- | -------------- | -------------- |
| **Channel** | Where they buy shapes what they see/consider | Brand/competition section |
| **Format/pack size** | Single-serve vs. sharing affects value perception | Concept evaluation (price probe) |
| **Frequency** | Daily habit vs. occasional treat = different value logic | Occasions section |
| **Social context** | Solo vs. shared affects which benefits matter | Concept evaluation or occasions |
| **Discovery** | How they find new products = launch implications | Brand/competition (if in scope) |

### Integration Principle
**Don't create a standalone "purchase behavior" section.** Weave these into existing sections as probes or sub-questions:
- Channel fits with brand/competition
- Format fits with price evaluation
- Frequency fits with occasion mapping

## 3. Sensory Vocabulary Building (Product Innovation)

For product-level research (food, beverage, personal care, etc.), actively build respondent vocabulary BEFORE evaluation. This gives the moderator personal reference points to probe against during concept reactions.

### When
After defining emotional territory (indulgence, treat, etc.), before brand/competition section.

### Format

| Method | Format |
| ------ | ------ |
| IDI | "Describe your ideal [product] texture in a few words" |
| FGD | Quick write exercise (2-3 words each), collect on flipchart |

### Why Active > Passive
**Passive listening** ("listen for texture words") misses respondents who don't spontaneously articulate sensory preferences.

**Active capture** ensures you can probe: "You said you wanted [酥脆/crispy] — does this concept deliver that?"

### What to Capture
- Texture
- Mouthfeel
- Aroma
- Visual cues
- Ingredient visibility

**Use respondent's own words, not category language.**

## Concept Evaluation Timing

| Method | Time per Concept | Questions per Concept | Total Concepts |
| ------ | ---------------- | --------------------- | -------------- |
| IDI | 4-5 min | 6-9 (mix of quick-fire and depth) | 4-6 max |
| FGD | 7-8 min | 4-6 + individual write | 4-6 max |

### Timing Reality Check
Not all questions are equal duration:
- **First-reaction (Q1-2)** and **purchase-intent (yes/no)**: ~30 sec
- **Believability, brand fit, competitive comparison probes**: 1-2 min each

Budget timing accordingly.

## Anti-Patterns

| Avoid | Do Instead |
| ----- | ---------- |
| Showing concepts without unaided capture | Always ask "what would you want?" before "here's what we made" |
| Passive texture/sensory listening | Active vocabulary building exercise before evaluation |
| Purchase intent without context | Probe channel, format, social context alongside price |
| All concept Qs treated as equal time | Budget 30s for quick-fire, 1-2 min for depth probes |
| Standalone "purchase behavior" section | Weave behavioral context into existing sections |
