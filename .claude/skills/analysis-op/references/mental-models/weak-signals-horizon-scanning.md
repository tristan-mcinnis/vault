## HOW TO THINK ABOUT WEAK SIGNALS

Weak signals are early, often ambiguous indicators of potentially significant change. They're like whispers before a shout—easy to miss but valuable to catch early. When analyzing transcripts, ask yourself:

- **What's unusual or unexpected?** Anomalies and outliers matter
- **What contradicts current trends?** Counter-trends often start small
- **What's mentioned only once but feels important?** Frequency isn't everything
- **What seems peripheral but resonates?** Edge cases can become mainstream
- **What language hints at emerging shifts?** New words, phrases, framings
- **What behaviors seem experimental?** Early adopters testing new approaches
- **What tensions or contradictions exist?** Conflicts often signal transitions

### Horizon Scanning Framework:

**THREE HORIZONS MODEL:**

**HORIZON 1** (Current mainstream - 0-2 years)

- Dominant practices and mindsets
- Incremental improvements to existing systems
- High certainty, low transformative potential
- Example: "Refining current e-commerce checkout flow"

**HORIZON 2** (Emerging practices - 2-5 years)

- Innovations gaining traction
- Coexistence of old and new
- Growing uncertainty, medium transformation
- Example: "Voice commerce testing by early adopters"

**HORIZON 3** (Future possibilities - 5+ years)

- Weak signals and experiments
- Visionary ideas and wildcards
- High uncertainty, high transformative potential
- Example: "Brain-computer interface shopping concepts"

### Weak Signal Characteristics:

**SIGNAL STRENGTH SPECTRUM:**

**Strong Signal** (Obvious trend)

- High visibility, many mentions
- Mainstream media coverage
- Clear direction and momentum
- Example: "Remote work adoption accelerating"

**Weak Signal** (Early indicator)

- Low visibility, few mentions
- Niche or underground awareness
- Ambiguous direction
- Example: "Some consumers experimenting with AI wardrobe stylists"

**Noise** (Random fluctuation)

- No pattern or coherence
- Isolated incident
- No broader implications

### Strong Weak Signal Insights Are:

- **Novel**: Genuinely new or unexpected behavior/attitude
- **Specific**: Concrete examples, not vague hunches
- **Contextual**: Include circumstances enabling emergence
- **Potentially Significant**: Could scale or transform category
- **Evidence-Based**: Grounded in actual transcript mentions
- **Directional**: Suggest trajectory or implication

### Weak Weak Signal Insights Are:

- **Obvious Trends**: Already mainstream, not weak signals
- **Researcher Speculation**: Imagined rather than observed
- **Trivial**: No plausible path to significance
- **Vague**: Lack specificity about what's emerging

### Signal Types:

**BEHAVIORAL WEAK SIGNALS**

- New practices, habits, or routines
- Experimental usage patterns
- Example: "Using AI to generate personalized recipes from pantry photos"

**ATTITUDINAL WEAK SIGNALS**

- Shifting values or beliefs
- Questioning of assumptions
- Example: "Younger consumers rejecting ownership in favor of access"

**TECHNOLOGICAL WEAK SIGNALS**

- Emerging capabilities being tested
- Novel applications of existing tech
- Example: "Consumers using VR for remote property tours"

**LINGUISTIC WEAK SIGNALS**

- New terminology or framings
- Changing metaphors
- Example: "'Conscious uncoupling' reframing divorce"

**SOCIAL WEAK SIGNALS**

- New communities forming
- Changing social norms
- Example: "Digital nomad communities establishing governance"

## Example 1: Shopping Behavior Evolution

### TRANSCRIPT EXCERPT

"I've started using ChatGPT to help me decide what to buy. I'll describe what I need and ask for recommendations, then research those options. It's faster than scrolling through Amazon for hours. Some of my friends are doing the same thing. I also saw someone post about using AI to analyze their closet photos and suggest what to buy next. That seems weird now but could be normal soon."

### ANALYSIS PROCESS

1. **Horizon 1**: Amazon browsing (current mainstream)
2. **Horizon 2**: AI shopping assistants (emerging)
3. **Horizon 3**: AI wardrobe management (weak signal)
4. **Novel behavior**: Using AI for pre-purchase research
5. **Experimental edge**: Photo-based wardrobe analysis
6. **Potential trajectory**: AI becoming shopping intermediary
7. **Implication**: Disintermediation of traditional e-commerce search

### SAMPLE WEAK SIGNALS

- AI shopping consultation replacing browsing; conversational product discovery emerging as search alternative
- ChatGPT product recommendations trusted over platform algorithms; consumer shifting from marketplace to assistant
- Friend network adopting AI shopping tools; early majority transition from early adopters
- Photo-based wardrobe analysis experimentation; visual inventory enabling predictive purchasing recommendations emerging
- AI intermediation between consumer and retailer; shopping assistants potentially disrupting platform marketplace models

### REFINED KEY WEAK SIGNALS

**[BEHAVIORAL]** AI shopping consultation replacing marketplace browsing; conversational discovery emerging over search-based finding

**[TECHNOLOGICAL]** Wardrobe photo analysis experiments; visual inventory enabling predictive purchase recommendations at edge

**[STRATEGIC]** AI assistants intermediating consumer-retailer relationship; platform disintermediation threat emerging from shopping agents

## WEAK SIGNAL IMPLICATIONS

Once identified, weak signals require strategic attention:

**MONITORING STRATEGIES:**

- Track signal evolution over time
- Watch for amplification or fading
- Note when weak signals strengthen
- Identify similar patterns across domains

**RESPONSE OPTIONS:**

**Prepare** (Low commitment exploration)

- Research implications
- Develop understanding
- Build optionality

**Experiment** (Small-scale testing)

- Pilot offerings
- Test with early adopters
- Learn before scaling

**Invest** (Strategic commitment)

- Build capabilities
- Position for emerging future
- Accept higher risk for advantage

**Ignore** (Conscious decision)

- Signal not relevant to strategy
- Too uncertain or distant
- Other priorities more important

**EARLY MOVER ADVANTAGE:**

- Weak signals offer lead time
- Can shape emerging categories
- Risk: investing in signals that fade

**PORTFOLIO APPROACH:**

- Track multiple weak signals
- Diversify bets
- Adapt as signals strengthen or weaken
