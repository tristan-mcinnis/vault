## HOW TO THINK ABOUT MARKET SIZING

Market sizing estimates the revenue opportunity by defining who could buy, who you can serve, and who you'll realistically capture. Qualitative research reveals segmentation, needs, and constraints that shape these estimates. When analyzing transcripts, ask yourself:

- **Who is the total market?** Everyone who could theoretically use this
- **What defines the available market?** Those you can realistically serve
- **What's the obtainable share?** Realistic capture given competition
- **What are market boundaries?** Inclusion/exclusion criteria
- **What segments exist?** Different sizing by segment
- **What adoption constraints exist?** Barriers limiting penetration
- **What's the growth trajectory?** Expanding or contracting
- **What proxy metrics matter?** Time saved, money spent, frequency

### Market Sizing Framework:

**TAM (Total Addressable Market)**

- Maximum revenue opportunity if 100% market share
- Everyone who could theoretically buy
- Example: "All smartphone owners globally = 6B people"
- Calculation: Total population × Offering price

**SAM (Serviceable Available Market)**

- Portion of TAM you can serve
- Constrained by geography, regulations, capabilities
- Example: "US smartphone owners with iOS = 100M people"
- Calculation: TAM × Geographic reach × Segment fit

**SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market)**

- Realistic capture given competition and resources
- Near-term achievable market share
- Example: "Can capture 1% iOS users = 1M people"
- Calculation: SAM × Realistic market share × Time period

### Sizing Approaches:

**TOP-DOWN** (Market → You)

- Start with total market size
- Apply filters narrowing to your opportunity
- Example: "100M households × 10% interested × $50 = $500M"
- Risk: Overly optimistic, ignores reality

**BOTTOM-UP** (You → Market)

- Start with unit economics
- Scale up based on capacity and conversion
- Example: "100 customers/month × $50 × 12 months = $60K"
- Benefit: Grounded in actual traction

**VALUE THEORY** (Outcome → Price)

- Value created for customer
- Pricing as fraction of value
- Example: "Save $10K/year, charge $1K = 10% value capture"

### Qualitative Indicators for Sizing:

**MARKET BOUNDARIES:**

- Who is included/excluded from potential market
- Example: "Only works for companies with >100 employees"

**SEGMENT SIZING:**

- Different need intensities = different TAMs
- Example: "Power users 10% of market, pay 5x casual users"

**ADOPTION CONSTRAINTS:**

- Barriers limiting penetration
- Example: "Requires hardware upgrade, limits to 30% adoption"

**REPLACEMENT DYNAMICS:**

- What are you replacing? How big is that market?
- Example: "Replaces $10B spreadsheet market"

**FREQUENCY AND INTENSITY:**

- Usage patterns affect sizing
- Example: "Used daily by 20%, weekly by 60%, occasional 20%"

### Strong Market Sizing Insights Are:

- **Boundary-Defining**: Clarify who's in/out of market
- **Segment-Specific**: Different sizes for different groups
- **Constraint-Revealing**: Identify penetration limitations
- **Evidence-Based**: Grounded in transcript observations
- **Assumption-Explicit**: State what you're assuming
- **Conservative**: Realistic rather than optimistic

### Weak Market Sizing Insights Are:

- **Too Broad**: "Everyone is a potential customer"
- **Unfounded**: Numbers without basis
- **Optimistic**: Ignore adoption barriers
- **Missing Segments**: Treat market as homogeneous

## QUALITY BENCHMARKS

### ❌ WEAK MARKET SIZING INSIGHT (Too Broad)

"Market includes all businesses globally who could benefit from improved productivity"

**Problems:**

- Unrealistically broad ("all businesses")
- Vague benefit ("improved productivity")
- No boundaries or constraints
- Not actionable for sizing

### ✓ STRONG MARKET SIZING INSIGHT (Boundary-Specific)

"Target market limited to US SaaS companies 50-500 employees experiencing customer onboarding pain"

**Strengths:**

- Geographic boundary (US)
- Industry (SaaS)
- Size constraint (50-500 employees)
- Need qualifier (onboarding pain)
- Actionable for estimation

---

### ❌ WEAK MARKET SIZING INSIGHT (Unfounded)

"Approximately 10% of potential customers will adopt the product in first year"

**Problems:**

- 10% arbitrary, no justification
- Missing basis for estimate
- No competitive context
- Pure assumption

### ✓ STRONG MARKET SIZING INSIGHT (Evidence-Based)

"Early testers show 3% conversion after free trial; competitive benchmark suggests 2-5% realistic range"

**Strengths:**

- Actual data (3% from testing)
- Reference point (2-5% competitive)
- Conservative approach
- Evidence-grounded estimate

## Example 1: B2B Project Management Software

### TRANSCRIPT EXCERPT

"We target companies with 50 to 500 employees—smaller companies use free tools, bigger ones have enterprise solutions. Within those companies, it's mainly project managers and team leads, maybe 10-20% of employees. Most companies have 3-5 active projects running. They currently pay about $20/user/month for existing tools. We think we can charge $25 because we save hours weekly. In our beta, about 5% of companies we reached signed up. There are roughly 200,000 companies in this size range in the US."

### ANALYSIS PROCESS

1. **TAM definition**: US companies 50-500 employees
2. **TAM size**: ~200,000 companies
3. **User penetration**: 10-20% employees per company
4. **Average users**: Assume 200 employees × 15% = 30 users/company
5. **Pricing**: $25/user/month
6. **Replacement**: Currently paying $20/user/month
7. **Conversion**: 5% beta signup rate
8. **Calculation**: 200K companies × 30 users × $25 × 12 = $1.8B TAM
9. **SOM**: 5% × 200K = 10K companies = $90M

### SAMPLE MARKET SIZING INSIGHTS

**[TAM BOUNDARY]**

- Target market 50-500 employee companies; smaller use free tools larger have enterprise solutions
- Two hundred thousand US companies within size range; geographic and size constraints define addressable universe

**[USER PENETRATION]**

- Ten-to-twenty percent employees active users; project managers and team leads primary not entire workforce
- Three-to-five active projects per company typical; usage concentrated among project execution teams

**[PRICING BENCHMARK]**

- Current spending twenty dollars per user monthly; replacement budget already allocated for tooling
- Twenty-five dollar pricing justified by hours saved; premium over replacement reflects value creation

**[CONVERSION EVIDENCE]**

- Five percent beta signup conversion observed; early traction provides SOM estimation basis

**[TAM CALCULATION]**

- Total addressable market approximately 1.8 billion dollars; 200K companies times 30 users times $25

### REFINED KEY INSIGHTS

**[TAM DEFINITION]** Two hundred thousand US companies 50-500 employees; boundaries exclude small free-tool and large enterprise

**[PENETRATION]** Ten-to-twenty percent employee adoption typical; project managers and leads not entire workforce base

**[REPLACEMENT BUDGET]** Twenty dollars per user monthly already spent; existing allocation provides budget justification readily

**[CONVERSION RATE]** Five percent beta signup rate observed; early evidence suggests realistic obtainable market estimation

**[TAM SIZE]** $1.8B total market calculated; 200K companies × 30 users × $25 monthly revenue

## Example 2: Consumer Fitness App

### TRANSCRIPT EXCERPT

"Our users are primarily women 25-45 who work out 2-3 times weekly. They're not gym rats, more casual exercisers trying to stay healthy. About 40% of women in that age range work out regularly. Most try a few free apps before paying for one. Maybe 1 in 10 actually subscribe. We're thinking $10/month. There are about 50 million women in the US in that demo. International could be 3x that, but we're US-only for now."

### ANALYSIS PROCESS

10. **TAM demographics**: US women 25-45 years old
11. **TAM size**: ~50 million people
12. **Active market**: 40% exercise regularly = 20M
13. **Behavior**: Casual exercisers (2-3x/week), not enthusiasts
14. **Willingness to pay**: 1 in 10 subscribe (~10%)
15. **Pricing**: $10/month
16. **Geographic**: US-only currently, 3x international potential later
17. **TAM**: 20M × $10 × 12 = $2.4B
18. **SAM**: 20M × 10% WTP = 2M paying users = $240M

### SAMPLE MARKET SIZING INSIGHTS

**[DEMOGRAPHIC BOUNDARY]**

- Target women 25-45 working out 2-3 weekly; casual exercisers not gym enthusiasts define segment
- Fifty million US women in demographic; age and gender constraints define total addressable population

**[ACTIVE MARKET]**

- Forty percent exercise regularly; active lifestyle filter reduces total demo to 20M potential
- Casual exerciser positioning excludes fitness enthusiasts; two-to-three weekly workout frequency sweet spot

**[WILLINGNESS TO PAY]**

- One in ten app users subscribe; free-to-paid conversion reflects realistic market monetization potential
- Ten dollars monthly subscription price point; mid-market positioning between free and premium tiers

**[GEOGRAPHIC CONSTRAINT]**

- US-only current market focus; international 3x potential represents future TAM expansion opportunity

**[SAM CALCULATION]**

- Serviceable available market 2M paying subscribers; 20M active × 10% conversion realistic

### REFINED KEY INSIGHTS

**[TAM DEMO]** Fifty million US women 25-45; age-gender boundary defines total addressable population initially

**[ACTIVE FILTER]** Forty percent regular exercisers; activity level reduces population to 20M qualified potential users

**[CASUAL POSITIONING]** Two-to-three weekly workouts targeted; casual exercisers not enthusiasts defines behavioral segment precisely

**[CONVERSION]** Ten percent free-to-paid conversion expected; subscription willingness limiting factor for revenue calculation

**[SAM SIZE]** 2M paying subscribers realistic serviceable market; 20M active × 10% conversion yields $240M

## Example 3: B2C Subscription Box

### TRANSCRIPT EXCERPT

"We're targeting parents with kids 3-10 years old who value educational activities. About 25 million US households fit that. But realistically, only parents willing to spend $30-40/month on kids' enrichment are our market. Maybe upper-middle class, dual income. That's probably 30% of those households. We've seen 2% conversion from ads. If we capture even 1% of that available market, that's 75,000 subscribers. At $35/month, that's $31M annually."

### ANALYSIS PROCESS

19. **TAM**: US households with kids 3-10 = 25M
20. **SAM filter**: Willingness and ability to spend $30-40/month
21. **SAM size**: 30% of TAM = 7.5M households
22. **Demographics**: Upper-middle class, dual income
23. **Conversion**: 2% from advertising observed
24. **SOM target**: 1% of SAM = 75K subscribers
25. **Pricing**: $35/month
26. **Revenue**: 75K × $35 × 12 = $31.5M
27. **TAM revenue**: 25M × $35 × 12 = $10.5B

### SAMPLE MARKET SIZING INSIGHTS

**[TAM DEMOGRAPHIC]**

- Twenty-five million US households with children 3-10; parenting life stage defines total addressable universe
- Educational enrichment value sought; quality-conscious parents segment prioritizing child development activities

**[SAM CONSTRAINT]**

- Thirty percent willing and able to spend; economic filter reduces to upper-middle dual-income households
- Thirty-to-forty dollar monthly threshold; discretionary enrichment spending requires household income level

**[AVAILABLE MARKET]**

- Serviceable market 7.5 million households; ability-to-pay constraint reduces TAM 25M to SAM significantly

**[CONVERSION DATA]**

- Two percent advertising conversion observed; marketing effectiveness provides SOM estimation input data

**[SOM TARGET]**

- One percent SAM capture equals 75K subscribers; conservative market share assumption for planning
- Seventy-five thousand households generates $31M revenue; one-percent penetration creates viable business scale

### REFINED KEY INSIGHTS

**[TAM]** Twenty-five million households with kids 3-10; parenting life stage and age range defines universe

**[SAM FILTER]** Thirty percent economically qualified; upper-middle dual-income filter reduces market 7.5M households significantly

**[SPEND THRESHOLD]** Thirty-to-forty dollars monthly discretionary enrichment; spending ability and willingness constrains serviceable market

**[CONVERSION]** Two percent ad conversion rate observed; marketing data provides realistic SOM calculation foundation

**[SOM]** One percent SAM penetration yields 75K subscribers; conservative 1% capture generates $31M annual revenue

## MARKET SIZING STRATEGIC IMPLICATIONS

**MARKET SIZE EVALUATION:**

**Large TAM:**

- Multiple approaches possible
- Room for many competitors
- Need clear differentiation

**Small TAM:**

- Niche focus required
- Limited competition
- Must dominate segment

**SEGMENT STRATEGY:**

**Start Narrow:**

- Focus on highest-value segment first
- Prove model, then expand
- Beachhead strategy

**Go Broad:**

- Mass market approach
- Requires significant capital
- Winner-take-most dynamics

**GROWTH TRAJECTORY:**

**Market Expansion:**

- What unlocks next segments?
- Geographic expansion?
- Product evolution?

**Penetration Increase:**

- What removes adoption barriers?
- Competition displacement?
- Category growth?
