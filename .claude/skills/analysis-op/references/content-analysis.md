# Content Analysis Reference

Extract market insights from user-generated content (social media posts, reviews, forums, etc.).

## Analysis Framework

For each piece of content, analyze:

| Dimension | Question | What to Capture |
|-----------|----------|-----------------|
| **Behavior** | What is the user doing/sharing? | Actions, activities, usage patterns |
| **Motivation** | Why are they sharing this? | Underlying needs, desires, goals |
| **Market Signals** | What products/services are featured? | Categories, brands, purchase considerations |
| **Cultural Values** | What aspirations/anxieties does this represent? | Lifestyle shifts, social dynamics |
| **Emotional Drivers** | What feelings motivate this? | Problems being solved, emotions expressed |
| **Opportunity** | What unmet needs exist? | White space, positioning opportunities |

## What to Flag

- **Products/brands mentioned**: Positive, negative, or neutral context
- **Purchase considerations**: Decision factors, barriers, trade-offs
- **Lifestyle indicators**: Aspirations, values, identity signals
- **Emotional language**: Frustrations, delights, anxieties
- **Social dynamics**: Who they're comparing to, seeking approval from

## Analysis Questions

1. **Consumer Behavior**: What is the user doing and why? What needs does this reveal?
2. **Market Signals**: What purchasing decisions are evident? What categories featured?
3. **Cultural Values**: What aspirations or anxieties does this represent?
4. **Emotional Drivers**: What feelings motivate this sharing? What problems being solved?
5. **Brand Opportunity**: What unmet needs exist? What would resonate?

## Quality Guidelines

**Good Analysis**:
- Focuses on **WHY** not just WHAT
- Identifies underlying motivations, not surface behaviors
- Connects to broader patterns and opportunities
- Specific and actionable insights

**Poor Analysis**:
- Just describes what's in the content
- Misses emotional and motivational dimensions
- Generic observations without strategic direction
- Assumes without evidence
