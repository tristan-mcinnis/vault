# Strategic Choice Frame

Every strategic recommendation must articulate what we will do, what we won't do, and why that tradeoff creates advantage.

## Elements

| Element | Description | Example |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| **DO** | Specific action with detail | "Build modular customization with 6 base options" |
| **DON'T** | Explicit rejection - what we won't do | "Offer pre-set variants or SKU proliferation" |
| **BECAUSE** | Strategic reasoning for tradeoff | "Mass customization drives 20% price premium" |
| **SACRIFICE** | What we lose | "Simplicity of inventory management" |
| **GAIN** | What we win that competitors cannot replicate | "Deep personalization moat, higher margins" |

## Application

Use this frame when:
- Elevating L2 insights to L3
- Framing final recommendations
- Presenting strategic options to clients

## Quality Check

A complete strategic choice:
1. Has explicit DO and DON'T (not just DO)
2. Explains the BECAUSE with evidence
3. Acknowledges SACRIFICE (shows rigor)
4. Articulates defensible GAIN

## Avoid

| Pattern | Problem |
|---------|---------|
| DO without DON'T | Not a real choice |
| BECAUSE without evidence | Assertion not insight |
| Ignoring SACRIFICE | Lacks credibility |
| Generic GAIN | No competitive advantage |
