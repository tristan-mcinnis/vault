# Tension Mapping

Tensions reveal strategic opportunities. Map competing forces to surface actionable contradictions.

## Tension Structure

| Element | Description | Example |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| **Force A** | First competing force | "Want to stand out" |
| **Force B** | Opposing force | "Fear of being judged" |
| **Manifestation** | How tension appears in behavior | "Buy bold items but only wear at home" |
| **Opportunity** | Strategic space in the tension | "Permission-granting messaging" |

## Format

```markdown
### Tension: Individuality vs Social Acceptance

**Force A**: Desire to express unique identity through product choices
**Force B**: Fear of standing out too much or being judged

**Manifestation**:
- Buy statement pieces but hesitate to wear in public
- Save bold choices for low-stakes contexts (home, close friends)
- Seek validation from peers before committing

**Evidence**: P2, P5, P7 — consistent pattern in 5/8 IDIs

**Opportunity**: Create "permission structures" that normalize bold choices
through social proof, celebrity validation, or community belonging.
```

## Identifying Tensions

Look for:
- **Contradictions**: What people say vs what they do
- **Competing desires**: "I want X but also Y"
- **Context shifts**: Behavior changes across situations
- **Hesitation points**: Where people pause or qualify
- **Trade-off language**: "On one hand... but on the other..."

## Tension Types

| Type | Pattern | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| **Want/Fear** | Desire blocked by anxiety | Want premium, fear overpaying |
| **Identity/Social** | Self-expression vs fitting in | Bold style, conformity pressure |
| **Now/Later** | Immediate vs delayed gratification | Impulse vs regret |
| **Rational/Emotional** | Logic vs feeling | Know it's unnecessary, want it anyway |

## Application

1. Extract tensions from findings (step 2 in analysis flow)
2. Use tensions to identify strategic opportunity spaces
3. Elevate tension-based insights to L3 with clear resolution

## Avoid

| Pattern | Problem |
|---------|---------|
| One-sided observations | Not a tension, just a finding |
| Manufactured tensions | Forcing contradiction that isn't there |
| Tension without evidence | Speculation not insight |
| Unresolved tensions | Identify the opportunity |
