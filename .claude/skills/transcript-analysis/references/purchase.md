# Purchase Drivers Reference

Extract factors that convert consideration into actual purchase from qualitative research data.

## Purchase Driver Framework

**TRIGGER → EVALUATION → TIPPING POINT → ACTION**
- What initiated consideration?
- What criteria were weighed?
- What converted consideration to purchase?
- What enabled transaction completion?

## What to Flag

- Conversion language: "That's when I...", "What made me finally...", "The reason I..."
- Urgency triggers: "I couldn't wait...", "I had to have it..."
- Risk reduction: "Knowing I could return it...", "The guarantee..."
- Social triggers: "My friend said...", "When I saw that..."

## Purchase Driver Categories

| Type | Definition | Examples |
|------|------------|----------|
| **Rational** | Logic-based | Value demonstration, feature necessity, price threshold |
| **Emotional** | Feeling-based | Desire, fear, excitement, self-reward |
| **Social** | Relationship-based | Recommendations, social proof, status |
| **Situational** | Context-based | Life events, timing, immediate need |
| **Promotional** | Offer-based | Discounts, trials, scarcity, guarantees |

## Quality Benchmarks

**Weak Driver (avoid)**:
- "Consumers purchase because they want good value and quality"
  - Universal, no specific trigger, not actionable

**Strong Driver (aim for)**:
- "Free return policy eliminated purchase risk; commitment-free trial drove immediate checkout versus continued research"
  - Specific feature, clear mechanism, behavioral outcome

**Weak Driver (avoid)**:
- "Brand reputation made them feel confident"
  - Post-rationalization, doesn't show actual trigger

**Strong Driver (aim for)**:
- "Friend's Instagram post showing results drove immediate purchase; social proof converted interest into action"
  - Specific trigger, timing, mechanism

## Common Tipping Points

**RISK REDUCTION**
- Free trials, money-back guarantees
- Reviews from trusted sources
- Return policies

**SOCIAL PROOF**
- Friend/family recommendations
- Visible results from known people
- Community validation

**URGENCY CREATION**
- Limited time offers
- Problem escalation
- Opportunity windows

**VALUE REFRAME**
- Cost of not buying exceeds price
- Problem severity justifies premium
- Failed alternatives escalate willingness to pay
