# Cross-Cultural Comparison Reference

Extract differences in behavior, preferences, values, and decision-making across cultures from qualitative research data.

## Cross-Cultural Analysis Framework

**WHAT DIFFERS?**
- Behaviors, values, meanings, communication styles
- Decision processes, social norms, expectations

**WHAT'S UNIVERSAL vs. CULTURALLY SPECIFIC?**
- Separate human needs from cultural expression

## What to Flag

- National/regional/ethnic differences mentioned
- Communication style contrasts (direct vs. indirect)
- Value conflicts in cross-cultural contexts
- Different meanings for same products/behaviors

## Hofstede's Cultural Dimensions

| Dimension | Low | High |
|-----------|-----|------|
| **Power Distance** | Equality, flat structures | Hierarchy, authority respect |
| **Individualism** | Collective, group harmony | Individual, self-focus |
| **Masculinity** | Cooperation, quality of life | Competition, achievement |
| **Uncertainty Avoidance** | Flexible, comfortable with ambiguity | Risk-averse, rule-following |
| **Long-term Orientation** | Immediate results, tradition | Patience, future focus |
| **Indulgence** | Discipline, restraint | Gratification, enjoyment |

## Communication Styles

**HIGH-CONTEXT** (Implicit)
- Meaning in context, relationships, non-verbal
- Indirect communication
- Examples: Japan, China, Arab countries

**LOW-CONTEXT** (Explicit)
- Meaning in words, direct statements
- Examples: USA, Germany, Scandinavia

## Quality Benchmarks

**Weak Insight (avoid)**:
- "Asian consumers are more focused on family values compared to Western individualism"
  - Broad stereotypes, no specific behaviors or contexts

**Strong Insight (aim for)**:
- "Japanese customers expect group consensus before purchase decisions; US customers decide individually prioritizing personal preference"
  - Specific cultures, concrete behavior, value difference implied
