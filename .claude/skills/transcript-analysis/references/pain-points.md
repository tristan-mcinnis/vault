# Pain Points Extraction Reference

Surface customer pain points, frustrations, and barriers from qualitative research.

## Pain Point Categories

| Category | Definition | Examples |
|----------|------------|----------|
| **Functional** | Task-related problems | "Can't find the right size", "Takes too long" |
| **Emotional** | Feeling-related problems | "Makes me feel guilty", "Creates anxiety" |
| **Process** | Journey/flow problems | "Too many steps", "Confusing navigation" |
| **Financial** | Cost/value problems | "Not worth the price", "Hidden fees" |
| **Social** | Perception problems | "Embarrassing to use", "Looks cheap" |

## What to Flag

- Complaints and frustrations
- Workarounds people mention
- Negative emotions expressed
- "Wish it would..." statements
- Abandonment moments

## For Each Pain Point, Capture

1. **The pain**: What's the problem?
2. **The context**: When/where does it occur?
3. **The impact**: What's the consequence?
4. **The frequency**: How often?
5. **Current workaround**: How do they cope?

## Severity Assessment

| Severity | Criteria |
|----------|----------|
| **Critical** | Causes abandonment, strong negative emotion, mentioned by multiple participants |
| **High** | Significant frustration, creates workarounds, affects satisfaction |
| **Medium** | Noticeable annoyance, tolerated but disliked |
| **Low** | Minor inconvenience, mentioned in passing |

## Quality Benchmarks

**Weak Pain Points (avoid)**:
- "Customers are unhappy" (too vague)
- "Price is too high" (surface-level)
- "Product isn't good enough" (not specific)

**Strong Pain Points (aim for)**:
- "Parents feel judged when serving packaged snacks at playdates, creating guilt that overshadows convenience benefits"
- "Premium positioning triggers skepticism because visible differentiation doesn't match price gap from standard options"
- "Resealable packaging never actually reseals properly, forcing transfer to separate containers or accepting staleness"
