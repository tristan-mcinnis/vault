# Social Influences Reference

Extract how other people shape decisions, behaviors, and beliefs from qualitative research data.

## Social Influence Framework

**WHO → HOW → WHAT EFFECT**
- Who is influencing? (Friends, family, influencers, strangers, communities)
- How is influence transmitted? (Recommendation, observation, pressure)
- What's the effect on behavior?

## What to Flag

- Influence statements: "My friend told me...", "Everyone says...", "I saw people using..."
- Social proof: "It's so popular...", "Sold out everywhere...", "Trending..."
- Judgment avoidance: "People would think...", "I'd look stupid if...", "My friends would judge..."
- Community norms: "In that group, you're supposed to...", "Everyone there has..."

## Social Influence Types

| Type | Definition | Example |
|------|------------|---------|
| **Informational** | Learning from others | Seeking recommendations, reading reviews |
| **Normative** | Conforming to expectations | Matching peer behavior, avoiding judgment |
| **Identification** | Aspiring to be like someone | Following influencers, emulating role models |
| **Compliance** | Direct persuasion | Acting on friend's recommendation |

## Influence Sources

| Source | Trust Level | Example |
|--------|-------------|---------|
| **Close ties** | Highest | Family, best friends |
| **Weak ties** | Medium | Coworkers, acquaintances |
| **Influencers** | Variable | Social media personalities |
| **Communities** | High within | Enthusiast groups, forums |
| **Strangers** | Lower | Reviews, ratings |

## Quality Benchmarks

**Weak Influence (avoid)**:
- "Consumers are influenced by social media when discovering products"
  - No specific platform, assumed without evidence

**Strong Influence (aim for)**:
- "TikTok product videos trigger impulse purchases; algorithm-served reviews override rational need assessment"
  - Specific platform, clear mechanism, behavioral impact

**Weak Influence (avoid)**:
- "Friends and family recommendations play an important role"
  - Generic, could apply to anything

**Strong Influence (aim for)**:
- "Sister's dermatology expertise overrides online reviews; family specialist status eliminates external research needs"
  - Specific relationship, credibility source, behavioral impact
