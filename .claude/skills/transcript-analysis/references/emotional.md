# Emotional Triggers Reference

Extract feelings that drive behavior and shape decisions from qualitative research data.

## Emotional Analysis Framework

**TRIGGER → EMOTION → BEHAVIOR**
- What prompts the feeling?
- What feeling emerges?
- How does it affect behavior?

**EXPLICIT vs. IMPLICIT**
- Explicit: "I feel...", "It makes me...", "I get so..."
- Implicit: Tone, word choice, metaphors

## What to Flag

- Emotion words: Happy, anxious, guilty, frustrated, proud
- Intensity markers: "Really", "so", "incredibly", "always"
- Contradictions: Behavior doesn't match stated feeling
- Emotional sequences: One feeling leading to another

## Emotional Trigger Categories

| Category | Emotions | What's at Stake |
|----------|----------|-----------------|
| **Identity** | Pride, shame, guilt, authenticity | Who I am/want to be |
| **Security** | Fear, anxiety, relief, trust | Safety and control |
| **Connection** | Belonging, exclusion, judgment | Relationships |
| **Autonomy** | Empowerment, helplessness, frustration | Agency and power |
| **Aspiration** | Hope, optimism, disappointment | Future self |

## Quality Benchmarks

**Weak Trigger (avoid)**:
- "Consumers want to feel happy and satisfied when they buy products"
  - Generic, no specific context, not actionable

**Strong Trigger (aim for)**:
- "Visible brand logos trigger social anxiety in budget-conscious consumers fearing judgment about spending"
  - Specific emotion, clear trigger, identifies segment, shows underlying fear

**Weak Trigger (avoid)**:
- "Parents feel grateful when brands help make their lives easier"
  - Assumed emotion, generic

**Strong Trigger (aim for)**:
- "Convenience solutions trigger parental guilt; saving time feels like cheating on caregiving responsibilities"
  - Counterintuitive, specific, identity connection, explains resistance

## Common Emotional Patterns

**GUILT-RESENTMENT PENDULUM**
- Indulging creates guilt, denying creates resentment
- No decision satisfies

**COMPARISON-INADEQUACY LOOP**
- Exposure to others' success triggers inadequacy
- Drives either striving or avoidance

**AUTHENTICITY ANXIETY**
- Shortcuts/help threaten identity as "real" [cook/parent/professional]
- Convenience feels like cheating

**VALUE-BEHAVIOR GAP**
- Know they "should" do X but don't
- Creates ongoing low-grade shame
