# Brand Associations Reference

Extract the mental connections and perceptions consumers have with brands from qualitative research.

## Brand Association Framework

| Type | Definition | Examples |
|------|------------|----------|
| **Functional** | What it does/delivers | Performance, quality, reliability |
| **Symbolic** | What it means/represents | Status, identity, values, lifestyle |
| **Experiential** | How it feels | Sensory qualities, emotions, memories |
| **User** | Who uses it | Perceived demographics, aspirational users |
| **Category** | Where it fits | Category ownership, competitive positioning |
| **Heritage** | Brand story | Origin myths, authenticity, history |

## What to Flag

- Brand names and reactions
- Imagery, metaphors, comparisons
- User profiles described
- Values and lifestyles associated
- Emotions and memories triggered

## For Each Association, Capture

1. **The brand**: Which brand is this about?
2. **The association**: What do they connect with it?
3. **Association type**: Functional/Symbolic/Experiential/User/Category/Heritage
4. **Strength**: Core (defining), Secondary (supporting), Peripheral (weak)
5. **Valence**: Positive, negative, or ambivalent

## Association Quality

**Strong associations are**:
- **Distinctive**: Unique to this brand
- **Emotionally charged**: Carry feeling
- **Behavioral**: Actually influence choice
- **Specific**: Concrete, vivid, detailed

**Weak associations are**:
- **Generic**: Could apply to any brand
- **Forced**: Inferred, not expressed
- **Aspirational**: What brand wants, not what consumers perceive

## Quality Benchmarks

**Weak Association (avoid)**:
- "Consumers think the brand is good quality and offers reliable products"
  - Too generic, could apply to anyone

**Strong Association (aim for)**:
- "Brand's weathered logo evokes grandfather's workbench; authenticity tied to generational hand-me-down tool imagery"
  - Specific sensory/visual, emotional (nostalgia), symbolic (heritage)
