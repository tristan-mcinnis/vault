# Cultural Influences Reference

Extract how shared beliefs, values, norms, and practices shape consumer behavior and decisions from qualitative research data.

## Cultural Analysis Framework

**SURFACE → MIDDLE → DEEP**
- Surface: Visible behaviors (what consumers do, buy, say)
- Middle: Shared norms (unwritten rules, expectations)
- Deep: Core values (fundamental beliefs about how world works)

## What to Flag

- Generational norms ("My parents' generation...", "People my age...")
- Social expectations ("You're supposed to...", "People notice if...")
- Value statements ("What matters is...", "You should care about...")
- Peer influence ("Everyone does...", "My friends all...")

## Cultural Influence Dimensions

| Dimension | Examples |
|-----------|----------|
| **Generational** | Boomer/Gen X/Millennial/Gen Z values |
| **Ethnic/Regional** | Heritage, traditions, geography |
| **Socioeconomic** | Class, status, aspirations |
| **Digital** | Social media norms, online communities |
| **Life Stage** | Parenthood, career, relationship status |

## Quality Benchmarks

**Weak Influence (avoid)**:
- "Hispanic consumers value family and tradition when making purchasing decisions"
  - Relies on stereotype, no specific evidence from transcript

**Strong Influence (aim for)**:
- "Sunday dinner approval required for purchases; family consensus cultural norm prevents individual decision authority"
  - Specific behavior, shows mechanism, evidence-based

**Weak Influence (avoid)**:
- "Millennials are influenced by social media when they discover new brands"
  - Generation cliche, no depth about how or why

**Strong Influence (aim for)**:
- "Performative consumption drives Gen Z purchases; products chosen for shareability not just personal utility"
  - Identifies cultural shift, underlying value, behavioral implication
