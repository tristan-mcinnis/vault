# Customer Journey Mapping Reference

Map customer experiences across journey stages from qualitative research data.

## Journey Framework

Standard stages (adapt as needed):

| Stage | Definition |
|-------|------------|
| **Awareness** | First learns about category/brand/product |
| **Consideration** | Actively evaluating options |
| **Decision** | Making the purchase choice |
| **Purchase** | Transaction and acquisition |
| **First Use** | Initial experience with product |
| **Ongoing Use** | Regular usage patterns |
| **Advocacy/Exit** | Recommending or switching |

## What to Identify

- Chronological narratives ("First I..., then I...")
- Decision points mentioned
- Touchpoints with brand/product
- Emotional moments (highs and lows)

## For Each Stage, Extract

1. **Actions**: What do they do at this stage?
2. **Touchpoints**: Where do they interact?
3. **Emotions**: How do they feel?
4. **Pain Points**: What's frustrating?
5. **Questions**: What do they need to know?
6. **Influencers**: Who/what shapes their behavior?

## Moments of Truth

Critical moments that disproportionately impact the experience:
- Make-or-break decision points
- High-emotion moments
- Unexpected delights or frustrations
- Points of no return

## Quality Guidelines

**Good Journey Mapping**:
- Grounded in actual participant narratives
- Captures emotional ups and downs
- Identifies non-obvious moments of truth
- Shows variation between segments
- Leads to actionable opportunities

**Poor Journey Mapping**:
- Generic stages without specifics
- Assumes linear path
- Ignores emotional dimension
- Misses critical micro-moments
- Too abstract to act on
