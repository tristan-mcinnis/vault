# Aspirations & Desires Reference

Extract future-state goals and ideal outcomes consumers yearn for from qualitative research data.

## Aspirations Analysis Framework

**CURRENT STATE → DESIRED STATE**
- What's the gap between where they are and where they want to be?
- What identity are they aspiring toward?

**MEANS vs. ENDS**
- What outcomes do they value most?
- Distinguish tools from actual desires

## What to Flag

- Future language: "I want to be...", "I wish I could...", "My goal is..."
- Identity aspirations: "The kind of person who..."
- Emotional payoffs sought
- External validation desires

## Aspiration Types

| Type | Definition | Example |
|------|------------|---------|
| **Identity** | Who they want to be | More competent, creative, authentic |
| **Lifestyle** | How they want to live | More freedom, balance, control |
| **Relationship** | Connection desires | Better parent, friend; belonging |
| **Achievement** | What they want to accomplish | Skills, career, recognition |
| **Experiential** | What they want to feel | Joy, peace, fulfillment |

## Quality Benchmarks

**Weak Aspiration (avoid)**:
- "Consumers want to live healthier lifestyles and feel better"
  - Too generic, no emotional depth, not actionable

**Strong Aspiration (aim for)**:
- "Aspire to effortlessly embody wellness without obsession; health as identity foundation not performance"
  - Specific quality, identity connection, reveals tension

**Weak Aspiration (avoid)**:
- "Busy parents want more time with their children"
  - Obvious, no insight into what that time represents

**Strong Aspiration (aim for)**:
- "Desire quality presence with children; aspire to be engaged parent not distracted caretaker"
  - Distinguishes quality from quantity, identity aspiration, emotional nuance
