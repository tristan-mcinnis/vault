# Competitor Comparison Reference

Extract competitive comparisons from qualitative research to understand relative positioning.

## Comparison Framework

| Type | Definition | Examples |
|------|------------|----------|
| **Functional** | Feature/performance | Quality, effectiveness, features |
| **Value** | Price/worth | Price positioning, cost-benefit |
| **Emotional** | Brand connection | Trust, authenticity, aspiration |
| **Experiential** | Customer journey | Service, usability, support |
| **Social** | Status/identity | What it says about the user |

## Competitive Positioning Types

| Position | Definition | Example |
|----------|------------|---------|
| **Aspirational** | Want to be like them | "Tesla if money weren't an issue" |
| **Peer** | Actively considered | "Went back and forth between Honda and Toyota" |
| **Fallback** | Settle for | "Lyft when Uber surge is too high" |
| **Dismissed** | Not in consideration | "Never buy Samsung - too invested in Apple" |

## What to Identify

- Moments when competitors are mentioned
- Comparison language: "Unlike X...", "Better than Y at...", "Not as good as Z"
- Trade-off discussions
- Switching triggers and barriers

## For Each Comparison, Capture

1. **Brands compared**: Which brands are being contrasted?
2. **Dimension**: What attribute drives the comparison?
3. **Winner**: Who comes out ahead on this dimension?
4. **Why it matters**: How does this affect decisions?
5. **Trade-offs**: What's sacrificed choosing one over another?

## Quality Benchmarks

**Weak Comparison (avoid)**:
- "Consumers think our brand is better than competitors in terms of overall quality"
  - No specific competitor, "quality" too vague

**Strong Comparison (aim for)**:
- "Brand A packaging feels premium versus Brand B's plastic; unboxing experience justifies price premium"
  - Names competitors, specific attribute, emotional dimension, links to outcome
