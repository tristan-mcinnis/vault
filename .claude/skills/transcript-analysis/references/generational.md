# Generational Cohort Analysis Reference

Extract differences in values, behaviors, preferences, and worldviews across generational cohorts from qualitative research data.

## Generational Analysis Framework

**COHORT vs. LIFECYCLE**
- Cohort effects: Generation-specific, persist across life (Millennials' student debt)
- Lifecycle effects: Age-related, every generation experiences (young adults = low income)

## What to Flag

- Generational comparisons: "My parents' generation...", "People my age..."
- Formative experiences referenced
- Value conflicts between generations
- Technology adoption differences
- Work/consumption attitude differences

## Generational Cohorts

| Generation | Born | Formative Era | Core Values |
|------------|------|---------------|-------------|
| **Silent** | 1928-1945 | Depression, WWII | Duty, sacrifice, tradition |
| **Boomers** | 1946-1964 | Post-war prosperity | Individualism, meritocracy, optimism |
| **Gen X** | 1965-1980 | Latchkey, MTV | Independence, skepticism, work-life balance |
| **Millennials** | 1981-1996 | Internet, Great Recession | Purpose, experience, authenticity |
| **Gen Z** | 1997-2012 | Social media, pandemic | Pragmatism, activism, mental health |
| **Gen Alpha** | 2013+ | AI, climate reality | Still forming |

## Key Generational Differences

**TECHNOLOGY**
- Boomers: Adopted workplace tech, skeptical of social
- Gen X: Bridges analog/digital
- Millennials: Smartphone/social natives
- Gen Z: Mobile-only, visual-first

**WORK**
- Boomers: Career loyalty, live to work
- Gen X: Work-life balance, pragmatic
- Millennials: Purpose-driven, collaborative
- Gen Z: Security-focused, side hustles

**CONSUMPTION**
- Boomers: Ownership, brand loyalty
- Gen X: Value-conscious, skeptical
- Millennials: Experience over things
- Gen Z: Sustainable, values-aligned

**COMMUNICATION**
- Boomers: Phone calls, email
- Gen X: Email, text
- Millennials: Text, social media
- Gen Z: Visual (TikTok), ephemeral

## Quality Benchmarks

**Weak Insight (avoid)**:
- "Millennials are lazy and entitled compared to hardworking Baby Boomers"
  - Judgmental, stereotype, ignores systemic differences

**Strong Insight (aim for)**:
- "Millennials prioritize workplace flexibility over salary; student debt and housing costs prevent traditional stability"
  - Specific preference, explanatory context, non-judgmental
