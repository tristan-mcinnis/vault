# Market Segmentation Reference

Extract distinct customer groups with different needs, behaviors, or characteristics from qualitative research data.

## Segmentation Analysis Framework

**MEANINGFUL DIFFERENCES**
- Look beyond demographics to behaviors, needs, and values
- Identify distinct decision criteria or priorities
- Find circumstances that create segments

## What to Flag

- Need statements that differ: "I need..." vs. "She needs..."
- Behavior contrasts: "I always... but my friend just..."
- Priority differences: "What matters most to me is..."
- Context dependencies: "When I'm [situation], I want..."

## Segmentation Bases

| Type | Definition | Example |
|------|------------|---------|
| **Needs-based** | Distinct unmet needs or JTBD | Convenience-seekers vs. quality-optimizers |
| **Behavioral** | Usage patterns, frequency | Weekly planners vs. spontaneous orderers |
| **Attitudinal** | Values, risk tolerance | Early adopters vs. skeptical pragmatists |
| **Demographic** | Age, income (least strategic alone) | Often correlates but doesn't explain |
| **Contextual** | Situations, life stage | New parents vs. experienced parents |

## Segment Evaluation Criteria

| Criterion | Question |
|-----------|----------|
| **Measurable** | Can you identify members and estimate size? |
| **Accessible** | Can you reach them through marketing? |
| **Substantial** | Large enough to justify different approach? |
| **Differentiable** | Would they value different benefits? |
| **Actionable** | Can you develop distinct offering? |

## Quality Benchmarks

**Weak Segment (avoid)**:
- "Market includes younger consumers 18-34 and older 35-54"
  - Arbitrary cutoffs, no behavioral basis

**Strong Segment (aim for)**:
- "Identity seekers use fashion as self-expression; outfit crafters prioritize uniqueness over trend conformity"
  - Distinct need, behavioral difference, actionable

**Weak Segment (avoid)**:
- "Some consumers care about quality while others focus on price"
  - Universal trade-off, not true segment

**Strong Segment (aim for)**:
- "Time-constrained dual-income parents sacrifice quality for speed; retirees prioritize experience over efficiency"
  - Clear contextual driver, specific value priority
