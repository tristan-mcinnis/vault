# Motivations Reference

Extract the underlying drivers that push or pull consumers toward certain actions from qualitative research data.

## Motivations Analysis Framework

**SURFACE → DEEPER → CORE**
- Surface: What they say ("It's healthier")
- Deeper: Real driver ("Makes me feel like a responsible parent")
- Core: Fundamental need ("I need to believe I'm a good parent")

**PUSH vs. PULL**
- Push: Moving away from pain (avoidance)
- Pull: Moving toward gain (approach)

## What to Flag

- Driver language: "Because...", "So that...", "I need to...", "In order to..."
- Emotional stakes: "I would hate if...", "I couldn't stand..."
- Identity statements: "I'm the type of person who..."
- Consequence language: "Without this, I would..."

## Motivation Categories

| Type | Definition | Examples |
|------|------------|----------|
| **Intrinsic** | Internal drivers | Growth, mastery, curiosity, self-expression |
| **Extrinsic** | External drivers | Recognition, status, rewards, approval |
| **Avoidance** | Moving away from | Fear, shame, regret, failure, loss |
| **Approach** | Moving toward | Achievement, pleasure, connection |
| **Identity** | Self-concept | Consistency with values, becoming ideal self |

## Quality Benchmarks

**Weak Motivation (avoid)**:
- "Consumers are motivated by getting good value for money"
  - Universal, no depth, not actionable

**Strong Motivation (aim for)**:
- "Motivated by visible value justification to partners; purchase choices must withstand spousal scrutiny"
  - Specific driver, reveals social dynamic, actionable

**Weak Motivation (avoid)**:
- "Parents want to provide healthy meals for their children"
  - Obvious, doesn't explain underlying emotional driver

**Strong Motivation (aim for)**:
- "Motivated by need to prove parental adequacy through nutrition; meal quality signals caregiving competence"
  - Identity driver, emotional stakes, explains charged topic
