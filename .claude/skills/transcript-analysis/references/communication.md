# Communication Preferences Reference

Extract how consumers prefer to receive and engage with brand communication from qualitative research data.

## Communication Analysis Framework

**CHANNEL + TIMING + TONE + CONTENT + FREQUENCY**
- Where do they want to hear from you?
- When are they receptive?
- How do they want to be addressed?
- What information do they value?
- How often is acceptable?

**PERSONALIZATION PARADOX**
- Helpful: Based on explicit choices, purchase history
- Creepy: Based on browsing behavior, inferred attributes
- The line: "Use what I told you, not what you watched me do"

## What to Flag

- Channel preferences: Email, SMS, app, phone, mail
- Frequency tolerance: Daily, weekly, never
- Timing sensitivity: Work hours, evenings, weekends
- Content preferences: Brief vs. detailed, visual vs. text
- Rejection triggers: "Annoying", "pushy", "too much"

## Preference Dimensions

| Dimension | Spectrum |
|-----------|----------|
| **Channel** | Email ↔ SMS ↔ App ↔ Phone ↔ Mail |
| **Timing** | Immediate ↔ Scheduled ↔ On-demand |
| **Tone** | Formal ↔ Casual, Expert ↔ Peer |
| **Content** | Brief ↔ Detailed, Visual ↔ Text |
| **Frequency** | Daily ↔ Weekly ↔ Monthly ↔ Never |
| **Personalization** | Generic ↔ Segmented ↔ Individual |

## Channel-Context Matrix

| Context | Preferred Channel | Why |
|---------|-------------------|-----|
| **Urgent/Security** | SMS + Call | Speed critical |
| **Transactional** | Email or SMS | Asynchronous, reference-able |
| **Promotional** | Email (low freq) | Easily ignored |
| **Updates** | App notification | Timely, contextual |
| **Complex Info** | Email | Space for detail |

## Frequency Tolerance

**HIGH TOLERANCE**
- Transactional (confirmations, shipping)
- Time-sensitive (deals ending, appointments)
- User-initiated (tracking, alerts)

**LOW TOLERANCE**
- Promotional content
- Educational/tips
- Generic announcements

**ZERO TOLERANCE**
- Calls during work hours
- Non-urgent push notifications
- Spam from data sharing

## Quality Benchmarks

**Weak Preference (avoid)**:
- "Consumers prefer email communications from brands"
  - No specificity, no context, no reasoning

**Strong Preference (aim for)**:
- "Order confirmation emails wanted immediately; promotional emails accepted weekly but daily sends trigger unsubscribes"
  - Distinguishes email types, specific timing, frequency threshold, behavioral consequence
