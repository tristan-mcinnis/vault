# Decision-Making Process Reference

Extract the actual steps, criteria, and mental models consumers use when making choices from qualitative research data.

## Decision Analysis Framework

**TRIGGER → SEARCH → EVALUATE → DECIDE → VALIDATE**
- What initiates the process?
- Where do they get information?
- What criteria matter (stated vs. revealed)?
- What tips the final decision?
- How do they justify the choice?

**STATED vs. ACTUAL PROCESS**
- How people think they decide vs. how they actually do
- Ideal process vs. shortcuts used

## What to Flag

- Decision triggers: "I realized I needed...", "When X happened..."
- Information sources: "I checked...", "I asked..."
- Criteria language: "It had to have...", "The most important thing..."
- Tipping points: "What made me decide was..."
- Heuristics/shortcuts: "I always just...", "My rule is..."

## Decision Complexity Spectrum

| Complexity | Process | Example |
|------------|---------|---------|
| **Low** | Trigger → Familiar option → Buy | Regular groceries |
| **Medium** | Trigger → Research → Compare → Select | Appliances |
| **High** | Trigger → Extended research → Stakeholder input → Reconsider → Validate → Buy | Major purchases, B2B |

## Decision Process Stages

**PROBLEM RECOGNITION**
- Trigger moment, need awareness

**INFORMATION SEARCH**
- Sources, depth, trusted validators

**EVALUATION CRITERIA**
- Must-haves, nice-to-haves, deal-breakers

**ALTERNATIVE COMPARISON**
- Consideration set, elimination process

**DECISION RESOLUTION**
- Tipping point, permission structures

**POST-DECISION VALIDATION**
- Justification to self/others, regret management

## Quality Benchmarks

**Weak Process (avoid)**:
- "Consumers research products online, compare options, and make purchase decisions"
  - Generic, no criteria, missing emotional/social factors

**Strong Process (aim for)**:
- "Narrows restaurant choices to partner-approved cuisines first; within that subset reviews determine selection order"
  - Shows sequence, hierarchy, stakeholder dynamics

**Weak Process (avoid)**:
- "Parents carefully evaluate nutrition labels when shopping"
  - Idealized, not actual behavior

**Strong Process (aim for)**:
- "Defaults to familiar brands under time pressure; only reads labels when trying alternatives"
  - Actual heuristic, context-dependent
