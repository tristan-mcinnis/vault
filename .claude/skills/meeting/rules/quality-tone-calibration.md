# Tone Calibration

Meeting documentation serves different audiences. Calibrate tone to context.

## Internal Documentation

For team debriefs, internal memos, and working notes:

| Principle | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| Direct and efficient | Get to the point, no preamble |
| Focus on actions and decisions | What was decided, what happens next |
| Minimal formality | Skip pleasantries, use shorthand |
| Honest assessment | Note concerns, risks, uncertainties |

**Example internal opening:**
> Three key decisions from debrief: (1) pivot recruitment to younger demo, (2) add sensory questions to DG, (3) push timeline 1 week.

## Client-Facing Documentation

For follow-up emails, shared summaries, and external artifacts:

| Principle | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| Professional but warm | Approachable, not stiff |
| Clear and scannable | Headers, bullets, bold key points |
| Friendly closing | Express appreciation, enthusiasm |
| Timely | Send within 24 hours |

**Example client opening:**
> Thank you for the productive kickoff today. Below is a summary of what we discussed and agreed upon.

## Timing Standards

| Audience | Deadline |
|----------|----------|
| Client-facing | Within 24 hours |
| Internal team | Within 48 hours (or end of day if urgent) |

## Avoid

| Pattern | Problem |
|---------|---------|
| Casual tone in client emails | Undermines credibility |
| Formal tone in internal notes | Wastes time, feels performative |
| Delayed client follow-ups | Signals disorganization |
| Generic closings ("Best regards") | Missed opportunity for warmth |
