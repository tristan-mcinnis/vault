# Meeting Types Framework

Different meeting types require different synthesis approaches. Match tone, focus, and follow-up to the meeting context.

## Type Matrix

| Type | Tone | Focus | Follow-up |
|------|------|-------|-----------|
| Client Kickoff | Professional | Objectives, timeline | Email within 24h |
| Client Status | Warm, efficient | Progress, blockers | Email within 24h |
| Team Debrief | Direct | Observations, patterns | Internal memo |
| Stakeholder Strategy | Strategic | Direction, decisions | Artifact only |

## Application Guide

### Client Kickoff
- Confirm understanding of objectives
- Capture timeline expectations explicitly
- Note any scope boundaries discussed
- Follow-up email establishes shared understanding

### Client Status
- Lead with progress made
- Surface blockers early and clearly
- Propose next steps with dates
- Keep email scannable

### Team Debrief
- Focus on what was learned, not what happened
- Extract patterns across observations
- Direct language, minimal formality
- Internal memo for team reference

### Stakeholder Strategy
- Capture directional decisions
- Note trade-offs discussed
- Document rationale, not just outcomes
- Artifact serves as decision record

## Avoid

| Pattern | Problem |
|---------|---------|
| Using casual tone for client kickoffs | Undermines professionalism |
| Delayed client follow-ups (>24h) | Signals disorganization |
| Overly formal internal debriefs | Wastes team's time |
| Missing context on strategic decisions | Decisions become untraceable |
