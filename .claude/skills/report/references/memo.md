# Executive Decision Memo Reference

Structure strategic recommendations for executive decision-making.

## Memo Purpose

Executive memos:
- Summarize findings for time-constrained executives
- Present clear recommendations for decision
- Support action with evidence
- Enable quick review and sign-off

## Memo Structure

| Section | Purpose | Length |
|---------|---------|--------|
| **Executive Summary** | Key finding and recommendation | 2-3 sentences |
| **Context** | Why this matters now | 1 paragraph |
| **Key Findings** | Evidence supporting recommendation | 3-5 bullets |
| **Recommendation** | Clear action to take | 1-2 sentences |
| **Rationale** | Why this recommendation | 3-4 bullets |
| **Next Steps** | Immediate actions | 3-5 bullets |
| **Risks & Mitigations** | What could go wrong | 2-3 bullets |

## Writing Guidelines

**Be Concise**:
- Lead with the answer, not the analysis
- One page maximum
- Use bullets, not paragraphs

**Be Actionable**:
- State recommendations clearly
- Include specific next steps
- Name decision points

**Be Balanced**:
- Acknowledge trade-offs
- Present alternatives considered
- Note risks and mitigations

## Quality Benchmarks

**Strong Memo**:
- Busy executive can understand in 2 minutes
- Recommendation is clear and specific
- Evidence supports the ask
- Next steps are concrete

**Weak Memo**:
- Buries recommendation in analysis
- Vague about what to do
- Too much background, not enough action
- Missing risks or alternatives

## Output Structure

```markdown
## Executive Decision Memo: [Topic]

**For**: [Decision Maker(s)]
**From**: [Author]
**Date**: [Today]
**Decision Required By**: [Date if applicable]

---

## Executive Summary

[2-3 sentences: What we found, what we recommend, why it matters]

---

## Context

[1 paragraph: Why this is on your desk now. What prompted this analysis.]

---

## Key Findings

1. **[Finding 1]**: [One sentence with evidence]
2. **[Finding 2]**: [One sentence with evidence]
3. **[Finding 3]**: [One sentence with evidence]

---

## Recommendation

**We recommend [specific action].**

This is preferred over [alternatives considered] because:
- [Reason 1]
- [Reason 2]
- [Reason 3]

---

## Next Steps

If approved:
1. [Immediate action] - Owner: [Name] - By: [Date]
2. [Short-term action] - Owner: [Name] - By: [Date]
3. [Medium-term action] - Owner: [Name] - By: [Date]

---

## Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|------|------------|------------|
| [Risk 1] | High/Med/Low | [How to address] |
| [Risk 2] | High/Med/Low | [How to address] |

---

## Appendix

[Link to full analysis if needed]
```

## Memo Types

### Decision Memo
Presents options with recommendation for executive choice.

### Information Memo
Shares findings without requiring decision.

### Action Memo
Requests approval for specific action.

### Status Memo
Updates on ongoing initiative.

## One-Pager Version

For very time-constrained executives:

```markdown
**Topic**: [What this is about]
**Ask**: [What you need from them]
**Key Finding**: [Most important insight in one sentence]
**Recommendation**: [What to do]
**Next Step**: [Immediate action if approved]
```
