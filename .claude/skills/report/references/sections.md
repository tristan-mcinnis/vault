# Report Sections Reference

Standard section frameworks for structuring research deliverables.

## Common Report Structures

### Strategic Research Report

| Section | Purpose | What to Include |
|---------|---------|-----------------|
| Executive Summary | Overview for busy readers | Key findings, recommendations |
| Context & Objectives | Why this research | Business context, research questions |
| Methodology | How we learned this | Approach, sample, limitations |
| Key Findings | What we learned | Organized by theme or question |
| Implications | What it means | Strategic and tactical implications |
| Recommendations | What to do | Prioritized actions |
| Appendix | Supporting detail | Full data, methodology detail |

### Insights Report

| Section | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| The Big Idea | One core insight that frames everything |
| Consumer Landscape | Who they are, what they want |
| Key Themes | Organized findings by theme |
| Tensions & Trade-offs | Contradictions and complexity |
| Opportunities | White space and positioning |
| Recommendations | How to act on insights |

### Category Analysis

| Section | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| Category Overview | Market size, dynamics, trends |
| Consumer Segments | Who buys, why, how |
| Competitive Landscape | Key players, positioning |
| Purchase Drivers | What influences decisions |
| Barriers | What prevents purchase |
| Opportunities | Unmet needs, white space |
| Strategic Implications | How to win |

## Analysis Dimensions

Standard dimensions for structuring multi-faceted analysis:

### Consumer Psychology Dimensions
1. **Motivations**: Why they act
2. **Barriers**: What stops them
3. **Decision Process**: How they choose
4. **Emotional Drivers**: What they feel
5. **Identity**: What it says about them

### Market Analysis Dimensions
1. **Market Signals**: Products, services, trends
2. **Consumer Segments**: Who buys what
3. **Competitive Dynamics**: Who wins why
4. **Cultural Context**: Values, norms, shifts
5. **Opportunity Spaces**: Unmet needs

### Brand Analysis Dimensions
1. **Perception**: How brand is seen
2. **Associations**: What comes to mind
3. **Differentiation**: What makes it unique
4. **Relevance**: Why it matters to consumers
5. **Positioning**: Where it sits vs. competitors

## Section Writing Guidelines

### Executive Summary
- Write last, after all findings complete
- One page maximum
- Lead with most important finding
- Include key recommendations
- Stand-alone readable

### Findings Sections
- One theme per section
- Lead with insight, support with evidence
- Use consistent structure across themes
- Include participant quotes
- Connect to business implications

### Recommendations
- Prioritized by impact and feasibility
- Specific and actionable
- Linked to findings
- Include rationale
- Note dependencies and timing
