# Horizontal Test

The "horizontal test" is the quality bar for all decision documents.

## Definition

A document passes the horizontal test when a senior leader reading **only the slide headlines** (not the body content) can understand:

1. What the strategic question was
2. What we learned
3. What we recommend
4. What's left to do

## Why It Matters

Senior leaders often:
- Flip through decks quickly before meetings
- Read headlines while half-listening to presenters
- Forward decks to other executives who weren't in the room

If headlines don't tell the story, the story gets lost.

## How to Test

1. Read only the headlines in sequence
2. Ask: "Does this tell a coherent story?"
3. Ask: "Would someone who missed the meeting understand our recommendation?"

## Examples

### Fails Horizontal Test ❌

```
1. Title
2. Background
3. Methodology
4. Consumer Insights
5. Concept Reactions
6. Competitive Landscape
7. Recommendations
8. Next Steps
```

Problem: These are topics, not findings. A reader learns nothing from the headlines.

### Passes Horizontal Test ✅

```
1. Chips Ahoy Premium Innovation — Oral Debrief
2. Can CA! Win Premium? Yes — Through Mood Uplift, Not Just Ingredients
3. The Trade-Up Formula: Familiar Trust + Elevated Experience
4. Why Not Copy Ferrero? CA!'s Advantage Is Approachable Premium
5. Mood Uplift = "A Little Escape" — Not Indulgence, Not Health
6. Hazelnut Leads, But Health Claims Undermine Credibility
7. CA! Wins at Work/Study — Loses in Social Sharing
8. Positioning Choice: Everyday Upgrade vs. Special Treat
9. The Bigger Gap: No One Owns "Premium But Not Pretentious"
10. Three Growth Paths — Path 1 (Additive) Is Safest
11. Base CA! = Filling Snack / Premium CA! = Mood Reset
12. Recommendations: Lead with Hazelnut, Drop Sugar Claim
13. Bottom Line: Familiar + Elevated = CA!'s Premium Formula
14. Work Still Needed: Quant Validation, Pricing, Packaging
```

A reader knows: CA! can win premium through mood uplift, hazelnut leads, work/study is the occasion, avoid health claims, hazelnut is the recommendation.

## Application

Before finalizing any decision document:

- [ ] Can I understand the story from headlines alone?
- [ ] Would a busy executive get the point in 30 seconds?
- [ ] Are all headlines findings, not topics?
- [ ] Is the recommendation clear from headlines?
