# Sample Design

Recruitment sample design for qualitative research proposals.

## Profile Structure

Each recruitment segment gets a named profile with three components:

```markdown
### Profile N: [Descriptive Name]

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Who they are** | [Demographics, life stage, identity] |
| **What they do** | [Behaviors, consumption, category engagement] |
| **Why we need them** | [What they uniquely contribute to the research] |
```

## Example Profiles

### Consumer Research

```markdown
### Profile 1: The Self-Reward Achiever

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Who they are** | Young professionals (25-30) who have recently achieved a significant career milestone. They view luxury watches as personal markers of achievement. |
| **What they do** | Work in finance, tech, consulting. Early-career high earners (¥300K-600K annually). Research purchases extensively, follow watch content creators. |
| **Why we need them** | They represent the "self-gifting" dimension of milestone purchases. They can articulate why a watch matters to them personally. |
```

### Cohort Research

```markdown
### Cohort 1: Young Affluent Newcomers (年轻富裕的高尔夫新入门者)

Aged 18-35, view golf as a symbol of aspiration but also as another new middle-class social sport—like tennis, frisbee, or skiing—that offers health, wellness, and socialization.
```

### Expert/KOL

```markdown
### Expert 1: Socio-cultural Expert

| Investigation Area | Milestones |
|-------------------|------------|
| **Role** | Investigate modern milestones in a fast-changing world where 'trend' and 'symbols' are shaped by memetic evolution |
| **Output** | Current literature on youth 'milestones' for a generation reshaping what 'progress' looks like |
```

## Sample Breakdown Table

Always include a summary table:

```markdown
### Sample Summary

| Dimension | Breakdown |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Total** | 8 participants |
| **Gender** | 4 men, 4 women (50/50) |
| **City** | 4 Shanghai, 4 Beijing |
| **Age** | x3 at 22-25, x5 at 26-32 |
| **Profiles** | x3 Self-Reward, x3 Gift Recipient, x2 Legacy Planner |
```

```markdown

| City | Cohort 1 | Cohort 2 | Cohort 3 | Total |
|------|----------|----------|----------|-------|

*4 participants per group = 24 total*
```

## Recruitment Criteria

List as bullets with clear include/exclude:

```markdown
### Recruitment Criteria

**Include:**
- Have recently purchased (within past 12 months), are actively planning to purchase, OR have received as a gift a luxury watch worth 40k+ CNY
- All participants must demonstrate genuine engagement with milestone watch purchasing
- Open-minded and articulate to explore abstract topics around culture, history, symbolism

**Exclude:**
- Consumers who only purchase online with no retail experience
- Category professionals (watch industry employees, retailers)
- Participated in similar research within past 6 months

**Affluence screen:**
- Property ownership, luxury watch ownership, auto ownership
- Annual household income exceeding 800k+ CNY
```

## City Selection

When multiple cities, explain rationale:

```markdown
### City Selection: Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou

Selection criteria:
- **Density of golf clubs, venues and places to play**
- **Social data on 'golf' and adjacent topics**
- **Strength of Jordan presence**
```

Or for global studies:

```markdown
### Strategic Markets

| Market | Rationale | Method |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| Japan | Strongest collector culture, vintage depth | 6 in-home visits |
| China | Fastest growing, platform dynamics | 2 FGDs |
| US | Cultural influence, streetwear crossover | 2 FGDs |
| Korea | Expert perspective only | 2 experts (waived) |
```

## Method-Specific Notes

### IDIs
- 8-12 typical for single-market
- 60-90 minutes standard
- Include pre-task where relevant

### FGDs
- 4-6 participants per group
- 90-120 minutes standard
- 2 groups minimum per segment for pattern validation

### Ethnography/In-home
- 6-8 typical
- 2-3 hours including shop-along
- Higher cost, richer data

### Expert Interviews
- 3-6 typical
- 45-60 minutes
- Mix of industry and cultural experts
