# Thought-starters

Thought-starters demonstrate IC's point of view on the problem before research begins. They are hypotheses, not conclusions.

## Purpose

1. Show the client we've done our homework
2. Frame the strategic tensions worth exploring
3. Give research direction without predetermining outcomes
4. Distinguish IC from commodity research providers

## Structure

Each thought-starter follows this pattern:

```markdown
### Thought-starter N: [Provocative Title]

**[Observation/Trend Statement]**

[2-3 paragraphs of evidence, data, or cultural context]

[Table or bullets with specific examples if relevant]

> Strategic Question: How might we [actionable question for the brand]?
```

## Examples

### Good Thought-starter

```markdown
### Thought-starter 2: Circle Symbolism Has Modern Currency

**Traditional Foundations**

圆 signifies perfection, reunion, full moon aesthetics

**Contemporary Reinterpretations**

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| 圆梦 | Fulfill dreams: personal achievement |
| 圈子 | Social circles: belonging, status, access |
| 蓝气球往上升 | Balloon rising: elevation and aspiration |

Signals **completeness, continuity, and arrival** for young professionals.

> Strategic Question: How can Ballon Bleu's circular form embody modern Chinese aspiration?
```

### What Makes It Work

- **Provocative title** — Not neutral, takes a stance
- **Evidence-based** — Includes specific terms, data, examples
- **Cultural insight** — Goes beyond surface observation
- **Strategic question** — Actionable, brand-specific, uses "How might we"

## Common Thought-starter Themes

### Market Dynamics
- Category shifts and disruptions
- Competitive positioning gaps
- Emerging formats or channels

### Consumer Tensions
- Generational differences
- Aspiration vs. reality gaps
- Cultural code conflicts

### Brand Opportunity
- Positioning white space
- Perception vs. reality
- Heritage reinterpretation

## Quantity

- **Minimum:** 2 thought-starters
- **Typical:** 3-4 thought-starters
- **Maximum:** 5 thought-starters (more dilutes impact)

## Tone

- Confident but not conclusive
- Evidence-based but not academic
- Provocative but not reckless
- Strategic but not prescriptive

## Anti-patterns

**Too neutral:**
> "Golf is becoming more popular in China."

**Too prescriptive:**
> "Jordan should launch indoor golf experiences."

**No strategic question:**
> [Ends without "How might we...?"]

**Too generic:**
> "Young consumers want authenticity."
