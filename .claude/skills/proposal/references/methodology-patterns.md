# Methodology Patterns

Common research approaches and phase structures for IC proposals.

## Standard Phase Patterns

### Two-Phase: Expert → Consumer

```
Phase 1: Expert Landscape          →  Phase 2: Consumer Immersion
(Market intelligence, hypotheses)     (Validation, depth)
```

Best for: Emerging categories, new markets, trend studies

### Three-Phase: Foundation → Validation → Synthesis

```
Phase 1: Cultural Foundation  →  Phase 2: Consumer Validation  →  Phase 3: Synthesis
(Desk research, semiotics)       (IDIs, FGDs)                     (Analysis, deliverables)
```

Best for: Brand positioning, cultural strategy, premium clients

### Two-Phase: Exploration → Activation

```
Phase 1: Consumer Exploration  →  Phase 2: Co-creation/Workshop
(IDIs, ethnography)               (Client workshop, prototyping)
```

Best for: Innovation, retail design, experience strategy

## Phase Components

### Phase 1: Foundation / Intelligence

**Activities:**
- Desk research and literature review
- Competitive landscape mapping
- Semiotic analysis (if relevant)
- Expert interviews (3-6)
- Stakeholder interviews (internal)
- Stimulus development for Phase 2

**Outputs:**
- Research hypotheses
- Discussion guide
- Stimulus materials
- Competitive map
- Initial frameworks

### Phase 2: Consumer Research

**Activities:**
- Pre-tasks (if applicable)
- IDIs (8-16 typical) or FGDs (4-8 groups)
- Ethnography/in-home visits
- Shop-alongs

**Outputs:**
- Topline findings
- Consumer journey maps
- Insight cards
- Validated hypotheses

### Phase 3: Synthesis & Delivery

**Activities:**
- Cross-source analysis
- Framework development
- Internal review
- Client presentation(s)

**Outputs:**
- Final report/deck
- Executive summary
- Supplementary materials (video, toolkit, etc.)

## Methodology Selection Guide

| Objective | Recommended Methods |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Understand a market/category | Desk research + Expert interviews + Consumer FGDs |
| Define a consumer muse/persona | Ethnography + IDIs + Synthesis workshop |
| Test positioning/concepts | Semiotics + Consumer IDIs with stimulus |
| Map a journey/experience | Shop-alongs + IDIs + Journey mapping |
| Generate innovation ideas | Consumer immersion + Co-creation workshop |
| Validate strategy | FGDs + Quantitative (if budget) |

## Specialist Methods

### Semiotics
When to use: Brand positioning, cultural translation, visual identity
Include: Scanning, landscaping, forecasting (RDE), plotting

### Ethnography
When to use: Understanding context, observing behavior vs. stated preference
Include: In-home visits, shop-alongs, accompanied activities

### Co-creation Workshops
When to use: Innovation, service design, experience strategy
Include: Cross-functional teams, prototyping, prioritization

### Pre-tasks
When to use: Priming participants, capturing context before interview
Types: Digital diaries, collage boards, journey mapping, photo missions

## Rationale Statements

Always explain *why* the methodology, not just *what*:

**Good:**
> This semiotic-led approach prioritizes cultural decoding over consumer feedback. We map the full symbolic territory first, then validate which meanings resonate with the target audience.

**Weak:**
> We will conduct semiotics and then interviews.
